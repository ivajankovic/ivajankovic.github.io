About Me

I am a PhD student and doctoral researcher at the UKRI AI Centre for Doctoral Training in Biomedical Innovation, based within the School of Informatics at the University of Edinburgh. My doctoral training framework is co-supervised by Dr. Filippo Menolascina and Dr. Vissarion Fisikopoulos.

My current research explores the intersection of computational biology, high-dimensional geometry, and control theory. I focus on developing learning algorithms for discovering synthetic lethal metabolic interventions, utilizing randomized geometric sampling algorithms to evaluate biological robustness and analyze the topological properties of gene regulatory networks.

Prior to joining the University of Edinburgh, I earned my Bachelor of Science with Honours in Biomedical Engineering from the Faculty of Technical Sciences at the University of Novi Sad.
