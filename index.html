Iva Janković

I am a doctoral researcher at the UKRI AI Centre for Doctoral Training in Biomedical Innovation, situated within the School of Informatics at the University of Edinburgh.

My core scientific work focuses on the development and application of hard mathematical algorithms, specifically within high-dimensional geometry, convex polytopes, and randomised geometric sampling. I am particularly interested in mapping graph topologies to system stability, and deploying uniform sampling primitives to analyze metabolic networks and topological properties of gene regulatory networks.

I am co-supervised by Dr. Filippo Menolascina and Dr. Vissarion Fisikopoulos.

Please navigate to the Research page for a comprehensive structural overview of my PhD framework regarding synthetic lethal metabolic interventions.
