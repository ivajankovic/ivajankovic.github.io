Bachelor Thesis Research

Pathophysiological Cardiovascular Dynamics: Real and Complex Fractional Order Models

Project Overview

This research focuses on developing highly accurate and interpretable mathematical models of the cardiovascular circulatory system to aid in the prediction of cardiovascular diseases. By analyzing clinical blood pressure data in the frequency domain via Bode plots, the study evaluates the cardiovascular system across four interconnected compartments. The core finding demonstrates that a streamlined 5-parameter fractional-order model captures heart dynamics with the same accuracy as a highly complex 20-parameter integer-order (ARX) model.

1. Bachelor Thesis Document

The comprehensive manuscript detailing the theoretical formulation of the non-integer models, the data acquisition protocols, and the non-parametric parameter identification process utilizing the particle swarm optimization (PSO) algorithm.

2. Conference Proceedings (MMSC 2024)

A peer-reviewed publication summarizing the comparative performance and frequency response analysis between standard integer-order models and the proposed fractional approach.

3. 3MT Presentation & Slide

An award-winning presentation translating the mathematical complexity into the intuitive analogy of a "musical equalizer." The accompanying slide visualizes the Bode plot (magnitude and phase) for left atrium–left ventricle dynamics, explicitly contrasting the opaque ARX difference equation $A(q)y(k) = B(q)u(k-d) + e(k)$ with the interpretable fractional transfer function $G(s)$. It highlights how critical frequencies ($\omega$) identify when dynamic changes occur, while the fractional orders ($\alpha$) define how strong those changes are.
