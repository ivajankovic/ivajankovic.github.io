Doctoral Research Framework

Learning Algorithms for Discovering Synthetic Lethal Metabolic Interventions.

1. Computational Geometry

Boundary sampling of convex polytopes

  • Billiard Walk & Shake-and-Bake Walk algorithms.
  • Sampling boundary facets of feasible regions.
  • Detects active constraints and collapse directions.

Geometric measures of biological robustness

  • Volume, anisotropy, directional widths.
  • Beyond FBA/FVA pointwise analysis.

2. Biocontrol & Dynamical Systems

Dynamical viability filtering for GRNs

  • Signed GRN topology: activation / repression.
  • Gauge-cooperativity as graph-computable certificate.
  • Polyhedral trapping blocks for attractor localisation.

Topology refinement

  • Minimal edge deletion or sign-flip.
  • Distance-to-viability score preserve high-confidence edges.

3. Game Theory & Reinforcement Learning

Game-theoretic intervention design

  • Interventions as players/actions.
  • Tumour vs healthy feasible spaces.
  • Potential game for stable multi-target strategies.

RL for sequential intervention discovery

  • State = current constrained polytope.
  • Action = gene/reaction constraint.
  • Reward = selective robustness loss.

4. Experimental Validation

Cancer-specific models

  • Reconstruct tumour and healthy-cell GEMs.
  • Test whether better models improve geometry-based discovery.

Experimental validation

  • Prioritise selective synthetic lethal candidates.
  • Validate with DepMap/gene essentiality data.
