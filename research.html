Bachelor Thesis Research

Pathophysiological Cardiovascular Dynamics: Real and Complex Fractional Order Models

Automatic Control Group, University of Novi Sad (July 2024 – April 2025)
Supervisor: Mirna Kapetina Radović

This research focuses on developing highly accurate and interpretable mathematical models of the cardiovascular circulatory system to aid in the prediction of cardiovascular diseases. By analyzing clinical blood pressure data in the frequency domain via Bode plots, the study evaluates the cardiovascular system across four interconnected compartments. The core finding demonstrates that a streamlined 5-parameter fractional-order model captures heart dynamics with the same accuracy as a highly complex 20-parameter integer-order (ARX) model.

The results of this research were successfully published and presented at the 3rd International Workshop on Mathematical Modeling and Scientific Computing (MMSC 2024). A comprehensive breakdown of the theoretical formulation and non-parametric parameter identification process is available in the original Bachelor Thesis (written in Serbian).

To communicate these findings to a broader audience, the core concepts were distilled into a 3 Minute Thesis (3MT) presentation, which won 2nd place at the BH Futures Foundation competition. The presentation analogizes the complex mathematics to a "musical equalizer", explicitly contrasting the opaque ARX difference equation $A(q)y(k) = B(q)u(k-d) + e(k)$ with the interpretable fractional transfer function $G(s)$. It highlights how critical frequencies ($\omega$) identify when dynamic changes occur, while the fractional orders ($\alpha$) define how strong those changes are.
