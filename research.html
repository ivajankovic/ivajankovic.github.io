PhD Doctoral Research Framework

Learning Algorithms for Discovering Synthetic Lethal Metabolic Interventions

An interdisciplinary approach combining computational geometry, control-theoretic viability filtering, game theory, and biological model validation to identify multi-target metabolic vulnerabilities in disease.

1. Computational Geometry

Boundary Sampling of Convex Polytopes

  • Billiard Walk & Shake-and-Bake Walk algorithms
  • Sampling boundary facets of feasible metabolic regions
  • Detecting active constraints and collapse directions
  • Supporting geometry-based vulnerability discovery

Geometric Measures of Biological Robustness

  • Volume, anisotropy, and directional widths
  • Boundary activity and active constraints
  • Evaluating metabolic flexibility vs. fragility
  • Moving beyond FBA/FVA pointwise analysis

Dynamic Volume Updating via Random Walks

  • Sequential constraint additions ($P_0 \supset P_1 \supset \dots \supset P_t$)
  • Sample reuse and annealed cuts
  • Scalable reward proxy for reinforcement learning

2. Biocontrol & Dynamical Systems

Dynamical Viability Filtering for GRNs

  • Signed Gene Regulatory Network (GRN) topology: activation / repression
  • Gauge-cooperativity as a graph-computable certificate
  • Compact attractors under biological boundedness
  • Polyhedral trapping blocks for attractor localization
  • Robustness margins under kinetic uncertainty

Topology Refinement

  • Framing failed GRNs as topology-editing problems
  • Minimal edge deletion or sign-flip optimization
  • Distance-to-viability score to preserve high-confidence biological edges
  • ML/RL searches for robust topology repairs

Proof-of-Concept Validation

  • Testing on canonical GRN motifs (mutual repression toggle, repressilator)
  • Incoherent feed-forward loops as failed cases
  • Simulating perturbations and comparing system robustness

3. Game Theory & Reinforcement Learning

Game-Theoretic Intervention Design

  • Interventions modeled as players and actions
  • Tumour vs. healthy feasible metabolic spaces
  • Utility formulation: $\text{Utility} = \text{Tumour Collapse} - \text{Healthy Damage} - \text{Cost}$
  • Potential games for stable multi-target strategies
  • Static baselines for intervention selection

RL for Sequential Intervention Discovery

  • State: Current constrained polytope
  • Action: Gene / reaction constraint addition
  • Reward: Selective robustness loss
  • Policy learning for ordered intervention combinations

4. Biomedical Impact & Validation

Cancer-Specific Models

  • Reconstructing tumour and healthy-cell Genome-Scale Metabolic Models (GEMs)
  • Comparing existing reconstruction methods
  • Testing whether improved model fidelity enhances geometry-based discovery

Experimental & Empirical Validation

  • Prioritizing selective synthetic lethal candidates
  • Validating predictions against DepMap and gene essentiality datasets
  • Experimental mission in collaboration with experimental research groups
